Blake Shelton | Music & Other Ventures

Music

Blake signed a recording deal with Giant Records in 2001.

Unfortunately, the label staff considered a song, I Wanna Talk About Me, unsuitable for Blake’s first single.

The same song was recorded by Toby Keith and peaked at number 1 on Billboard.

Nonetheless, lucky for him his first single, Austin, spent five weeks on the Billboard Hot Country chart at the number one spot.

After Giant Records was shuttered down, Blake was transferred to Warner Bros.

His debut self-titled album Blake Shelton was immensely successful and received platinum certification.

Some of his number one hit singles include Some Beach, Hillybilly Bone, Honey Bee, Over, Every Time Hear that Song, A Guy With a Girl, and Came Here to Forget.

He has sold tens of millions of records worldwide with more than 1.7 billion digital streams.

Other Pursuits

Shelton has invested his hard-earned cash in several pursuits.

He owns an entertainment complex and franchise of restaurants called Ole Red.

He started the first Ole Red in his hometown of Tishomingo, Oklahoma, in September 2017.

In total, Ole Red has four branches in different parts of the United States.

A Nashville location of Ole Red was opened in May 2018, Gatlinburg in March 2019, and Orlando, Florida, in May 2020.
