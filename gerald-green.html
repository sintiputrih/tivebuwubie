Gerald Green - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Married, Nationality, Facts

Table of Biography

Gerald Green is an American Basketball Player who currently plays in the G-League for Rio Grande Valley Vipers. Gerald Green is mostly for his Dunks tricks as he has won the 2005 McDonald’s All-American Slam Dunk Contest, and the 2007 NBA Slam  Dunk Contest whilst finishing runner-up in the 2008 NBA Slam Dunk Contest.

Early Life

Gerald Green was born on January 26, 1986, in  Houston, Texas, USA. Likewise, he is 38 years old. He was born to a mother Brenda Green and a father whose name is also Gerald Green. His father is a professional firefighter. He has 3 siblings, two sisters, Terika Green and Delonya Green, and a brother Garlon Green who is also a Basketball Professional overseas.

He tore his right ring finger whilst playing basketball when he was in 6th grade. The accident occurred when he was attempting to dunk on the makeshift hoop and his finger got stuck on the nail with amputation as the only option.

Gerald started playing basketball only in his high school sophomore year. He played junior varsity for J. Frank Doble High School. He got cut from the team due to his academic issues. Soon he transferred to a charter school, Gulf Shores Academy where he repeated his grade. Green was named an All-American.

He was also the high scorer in McDonald’s All-American Game. He also won the 2005 McDonald’s All-American Slam Dunk Contest defeating Josh Roberts. Green originally committed to Oklahoma State University but he later decided to enter the NBA Draft directly. He was one of the last players to enter the NBA directly out of high school.

Gerald Green – Professional Career

Gerald Green mostly played as a Shooting Guard/Small forward throughout his career. Green was drafted by the Boston Celtics with the 18th pick. He played 2  seasons for Boston before he became part of the Kevin Garnett trade in 2007. After the trade to the Minnesota Timberwolves, green found himself stuck on the bench. He won the 2007  NBA Slam Dunk Contest. Green requested a trade from the Wolves. He was traded to his hometown team Houston Rockets but he was shortly released by the Rockets.

Green also played overseas for teams like Lokomotiv Kuban, Foshan Dralions, and Krasnye Krylia but made a return to NBA on December 8 when he signed with Los Angeles but he was waived by the Lakers on Dec 22. He signed with the Los Angeles D -D-Fenders of the NBA Development  League. He was awarded the NBA D-League All-Star Game Most Valuable Player at NBA D-League All-Star.

In  2012, he signed a 10-day contract with the New Jersey Nets where his dunk against Houston became viral and is one of the best dunks of all time. He got signed by Jersey for the rest of the season. He also played for the Miami Heat, Indiana Pacers, and Phoenix Suns before his second stint with Boston in 2016. In 2018, Green resigned from Houston and played for 2 seasons.

He retired in October of the 2020-21 season and joined the Houston coaching staff. Green came out of retirement on January 28 and signed for G-League team Rio Grande Valley Vipers.
