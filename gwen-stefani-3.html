Career

Gwen Stefani embarked on the journey towards her career in 1986 by joining the newly-founded music band titled “No Doubt” as a lead vocalist and songwriter. After a few months, the band acclaimed huge fame throughout the world.

In 1992, the band released its first self-titled album. After 3 years in 1995, it released two albums titled “The Beacon Street Collection” and the Tragic Kingdom”. “Return of Saturn”, “Rock Steady” and “Push and Shove” are other albums released by the band.

She also made her solo albums. Her debuted solo album titled “Love. Angel. Music. Baby.” was launched in 2004. Her other solo albums include “The Sweet Escape”, “This Is What the Truth Feels Like”, “You Make It Feel Like Christmas” and some untitled albums.

She has also worked in various television shows and films. From 1996-2016, she served as a musical guest in the American sketch comedy show “Saturday Night Live” and also appeared in the American sitcom “King of the Hill”, Teen drama television series  “Dawson’s Creek”, and comedy series “Portlandia” with “No Doubt”. She was featured in the documentaries such as “Everyday Sunshine: The Story of Fishbone”, “The Defiant”, “Through the Eyes of Faith”, “Fashion Rocks” and “Brain Fart”.

She also portrayed the character “Jean Harlow” in the 2004 American epic biographical drama film “The Aviator”. Furthermore, she made a cameo appearance in the action-comedy film “Zoolander” in 2001 and lent her voice to the video game titled “Malice”.
