Hanneli Rupert Biography

Hanneli Rupert is a South African entrepreneur and the founder and director of Okapi, a luxurious handbag line. She is also the Creative Director and Buyer of Merchants on Long.

Hanneli Rupert Education

  • 2004 – 2007: Bachelor of Arts in Painting from the Wimbledon School of Arts.

Hanneli Rupert Career

She trained as a painter, which she pursued professionally for several years before launching Okapi and MoL. Other career highlights include speaking at the International Herald Tribune Luxury conference 2014 and being nominated as a member of the BOF (Business of Fashion) 500 in both 2013 and 2014. Hanneli also sits on the Tate International Council and is a trustee of the South African children’s education charity Imibala.

  • August 2008 – Present: Founder, CEO and Creative Director of Okapi.
  • 2010 – Present: Creative Director and Buyer of Merchants on Long.
  • February 2014 – Present: Young Global Leader at World Economic Forum.
  • January 2009 – August 2011: DJ at 2oceansvibe Media.
  • 2008 – 2011: Event Organiser at L’Ormarins Queens Plate.
  • March 2008 – August 2008: Intern at Athens Biennale.
  • January 2002: Art Therapy Assistant at Imibala.

Okapi Luxurious Handbag Line

The bags are made entirely on the African continent from locally sourced materials. She discovered other brands were doing similar things, and she decided to open an entirely African concept store on the continent, hence Merchants on Long was born in Cape Town.

Okapi is named from an era during which Okapi was still thought to be a magical animal, “the African unicorn.” The brand encompasses elements of Art Nouveau, African mysticism and organic elements taking inspiration from nature and ritual.
