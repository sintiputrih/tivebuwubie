Wayne Bertram Williams (born May 27, 1958) is a convicted killer who has been accused of being responsible for the majority of the Atlanta Child Murders that occurred between 1979 and 1981. In 1982, he was prosecuted and found guilty of the murders of two adult males, and he was sentenced to life in prison. Even though the Atlanta Police Department stated that Williams was guilty for at least 23 of the 29 child killings, he has never been charged or tried for any of them since his conviction. Williams’s guilt has been called into question by a number of parties, and he himself continues to assert his innocence.
