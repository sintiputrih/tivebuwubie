Santiago Cruz Wife: Meet María Paz Mateus – Santiago Cruz was born in Ibagué, Colombia, to a mother, two grandmothers, and ten aunts.

After living in his hometown for 17 years and spending his adolescence between studies and military service, he moved to Colombia’s capital to study Finance and International Relations.

Despite this, Cruz was always certain that he would devote himself entirely to music, as it was one of his great passions.

Cruz released his first album, Solo hasta hoy, in 2003. His breakthrough came with the single “Una y otra vez.” This album also allowed him to meet and perform alongside many famous musicians, including Alejandro Sanz, Miguel Bosé, and Franco de Vita.

Cruz’s second album, Sentidos, was released in 2006 on the independent label “Equilibrio Producciones.” This album was co-produced by Julián Vila, a music producer, and Fredy Ardila, a concert producer.

Cruz released his third album, Cruce de caminos, under the Sony Music label in 2009. He described the album’s songs as coming from his soul.

Cruz released his fourth studio album, A quien corresponda, in 2012. This album contains 13 songs that were written during his previous album’s concerts and trips.

Cruz returned in 2014 with his fifth studio album, Equilibrio, which went on sale in digital and physical formats on September 23 in Colombia and several Latin American countries after several months of work, a long trip to Spain, and many compositions.

Cruz, Elsa y Elmar, and Pedro Cap released the album Trenes, Aviones, y Viajes Interplanetarios in 2017. This album was nominated for a Latin GRAMMY in the category of “Best Singer-Songwriter Album.”

This album included the single “Contar hasta 3, O hasta 10,” which focused on women’s rights and the feminist movement.

Cruz released his album Elementales in 2019. Cruz also released his most recent single “Baja la Guardia,” one of his most powerful songs, which was produced by Cachorro López and accompanied by Andrés Cepeda.

Cruz was appointed Goodwill Ambassador for UNDP by the United Nations Development Program (UNDP) representative in Colombia, Bruno Moro, in 2011.

His first UNDP assignment began in October 2010 at the Expopaz peacebuilding festival in Bogota. Cruz’s new responsibilities would include combating poverty and violence against women “in a continuous call for peace and reconciliation among Colombians.”
