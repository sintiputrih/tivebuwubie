Who is Nico Collins?

Nico Collins, an American football wide receiver with the Houston Texans of the National Football League (NFL), was born on March 19, 1999. He was a football player at Michigan University. At Clay-Chalkville High School in Clay, Alabama, Collins played wide receiver.

He caught 60 receptions for 1,103 yards and 16 touchdowns as a junior. A senior year with 43 passes for 865 yards and 9 touchdowns came next. Collins pledged in February 2017 to play football for Michigan rather than Georgia or Alabama.

Nico Collins caught his first college pass on October 12, 2017, a 12-yard pass against Rutgers University. He saw action in two games as a freshman and had three receptions for 27 yards. In 2018, he enjoyed a breakthrough season as a sophomore. Against Ohio State, he hauled in four passes for a career-high 91 yards and two touchdowns.

With 38 receptions for 632 yards and six touchdowns, he was the Most Improved Offensive Player after the season. Collins had a career-high 165 receiving yards and three touchdowns against Indiana on November 23, 2019. Despite sharing the field alongside Ronnie Bell, Tarik Black, and Donovan Peoples-Jones, Collins led the club in receiving yards in 2019 with 729 from 37 grabs and seven touchdowns.

The Houston Texans selected Collins 89th overall in the third round of the 2021 NFL Draft. Collins formally signed with the Texans on May 12, 2021. Collins was named as the starting wide receiver going into his first NFL training camp, alongside veterans Chris Conley and Brandin Cooks.

After the NFL preseason, Collins, Cooks, and Conley were selected as starting wide receivers by head coach David Culley. Against the Jacksonville Jaguars in the Texans’ Week 1 triumph, Collins made his NFL debut and first career start. He noted one reception for seven yards.

Nico Collins played the entire first half of the Texans’ Week 2 loss against the Cleveland Browns, registering one reception for 32 yards before leaving the game early due to a shoulder injury. On September 21, 2021, Collins was placed on injured reserve. On October 16, 2021, he was activated and made a comeback to start the Texans’ Week 6 loss to the Indianapolis Colts.

Nico Collins grabbed his first touchdown pass in the NFL in the Texans’ Week 16 victory over the Los Angeles Chargers, in addition to recording five receptions for 69 yards against the Seattle Seahawks in Week 14. Ultimately, Collins played in just over half of the Texans’ offensive plays toward the end of his rookie campaign, making appearances in 14 games (8 starts) and hauling in 33 receptions for 446 yards and one score.

Collins was the Texans’ second-best receiver going into the 2022 campaign, behind Brandin Cooks. Throughout 10 games and seven starts, he concluded the season with 37 receptions for 481 yards and two touchdowns. Due to a foot ailment, Collins was placed on season-ending injured reserve on December 23, 2022.

The Texans fell 20–31 to the Indianapolis Colts in Week 2, despite Collins finishing with 146 receiving yards and a score. Collins recorded new career highs for the most receptions in a single game with 168 receiving yards and two touchdowns in a Week 4 victory over the Pittsburgh Steelers.
