Dan Rather | Career

Dan Rather started his media career as an Associated Press correspondent in 1950.

He went on to work for United Press, the Houston Chronicle, and many Texas radio stations.

Rather worked as the play-by-play commentator for the University of Houston football team for four seasons near the end of the 1950s.

He started his television journalism as a reporter for the ABC Houston affiliate KTRK-TV before being appointed to the CBS Houston affiliate KHOU-TV director.

Dan moved to New York city in 1962 for a trial term with CBS. He was then promoted to head of CBS’s Southwest bureau in Dallas and then to head of the Southern bureau in New Orleans.

Rather covered several big issues throughout his time as a CBS news anchor.

The Challenger Space Shuttle catastrophe, the Iran-Contra affair, the Soviet-Afghan War, and the 2003 Iraq invasion are among them.

After getting fired from the show, Dan joined AXS TV to produce the weekly one-hour news show “Dan Rather Reports.”
