Early Life & Vocation

Gloria Allred was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, the USA on July 3, 1941, as Gloria Bloom. She was born into a working-class family of Philadelphia, who is Jewish. Her mother Stella was a housewife, and father Morris worked as a salesman. 

Gloria attended the University of Pennsylvania after graduating from the Philadelphia High School for Girls. She earned a bachelor's degree with honors in English sub-1963. She wrote her honors thesis on black writers. 

She became a teacher after earning a master's degree and moved to Los Angeles, California. She also joined the Los Angeles Teachers Association and taught at Fremont High School and Jordan High School.

Gloria has been represented a wide variety of clients in civil rights and has involved women's rights, sexual harassment, wrongful termination, sexual harassment, and discrimination.
