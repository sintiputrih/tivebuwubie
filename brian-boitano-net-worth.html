Brian Boitano | Lifestyle and Vacations

Lifestyle

Brian was born in Mountain View, California, on October 22, 1963. He is the son of Louis Boitano and Donna.

As the youngest of four children, he was born into an upper-middle-class Italian American family in Sunnyvale, California. 

The successful figure skater completed his high school studies at Marian A. Petersen High School in Sunnyvale.

Brian was a big reader as a child, and his favorite sports were roller skating, skiing, tennis, biking, and baseball.  However, he got more interested in skating after the age of eight.

The skater has traveled worldwide for his skating career and is fascinated by diverse cultures and cuisines. He now gets the same profound satisfaction from cooking and entertaining as skating.

Similarly, his travels have influenced his cooking approach, using his Italian heritage. His cookbook includes personal stories and dishes inspired by treasured family recipes.

He loves to be around his friends and families, and good food has long been an important element of every gathering.

Charity

Brian is a generous person. His dream is to give back to the skating community.

Since its start, more than 10,000 youngsters aged 5 to 15 have participated in the program. Brian is an active participant in the charity, which is now in its 20th season.

Skating, T.V. Shows, Investments & Book Publication

Skating

Brian began his figure skating career when he was eight years old in 1972. After seeing an “Ice Follies” performance, he changed his roller skates for ice skates and enrolled in group lessons with Linda Leaver.

She saw his ability right away and proposed private sessions. Thus began a wonderful 30-year partnership that is still going strong, with Linda also functioning as Brian’s manager.

He won the US Junior Men’s Championship at 14 while still in high school. Brian became the first skater to perform all six distinct triple leaps in a World Championship at the age of 19.

Likewise, in 1984, he finished fifth in the Olympic Winter Games in Sarajevo, and in 1985, he won the first of four consecutive U.S. Men’s Titles.

Brian left his professional career shortly after these achievements.

However, he returned to figure skating in 1993, according to the “Boitano Rule,” which allowed retired professional athletes to compete as “amateurs,” which was approved by the International Skating Union in 1993.

Brian won the silver medal at Skate America in 1994. He also earned a spot in the 1994 Winter Olympics in Lillehammer, Norway, where he finished sixth. He then retired from skating permanently.

T.V. Shows

Brian was the first American male athlete to have his network television special, “Canvas of Ice,” which aired nationally on the ABC Network and was broadcast worldwide.

For his role in the film “Carmen on Ice,” Brian received a Prime-Time Emmy Award, television’s highest honor.

Similarly, he traveled the country with Champions On Ice for 15 years, headlining 25 national tours. Since 1990, Brian has offered expert commentary and hosted televised skating events for the ABC, NBC, and Turner networks.

Similarly, Brian’s show “What Would Brian Boitano Make?” was aired by  Food Network on August 23, 2009. This show takes its name and opening musical theme from the South Park: Bigger, Longer & Uncut song.

Brian prepares meals for his friends on the show.

Likewise, he had an appearance in Season 18 of the reality show Hell’s Kitchen when he co-judged the team challenge with Gordon Ramsay and Traci Des Jardins in the episode “Hell Freezes Over.”

He also had a show called The Brian Boitano Project on HGTV in 2014. It showed him purchasing and refurnishing his ancestors’ houses in Italy.

Investments

Brian co-founded White Canvas Productions in 1995; it produces live and televised figure skating programs. There have been over 30 White Canvas specials.

Similarly, the successful figure skater owns a hotel named Boitano’s lounge, located in Lincoln, Nebraska. It was awarded “Best Hotel Lobby” in 2020 by Boutique Design Magazine.

He plans to open two other boutiques Kindler Hotels, in Kansas City, Missouri, and Baltimore, Maryland. Here, he wants to serve locally inspired cuisine.

Besides, he is also exploring being an executive producer of an upcoming Netflix movie called “Take the Ice.”

Book Publication

Brian has published a book titled Boitano’s Edge: Inside the Real World of Figure Skating in November 1997. It was sold out in its third printing and is widely regarded as one of the best skating books ever written.

Similarly, Brian has written a cookbook titled, What Would Brian Boitano Make? Fresh And Fun Recipes in 2013. It is a fun and simple cookbook to read.

Career

Brian has won more than 50 medals in all, including 23 international gold medals, two World Championships, two Pro/Am titles, 16 professional titles, four U.S. National titles, and the Olympic Gold Medal.

In 1982, Brian became the first American to land a triple axel. In 1987, he invented his signature technique, the Tano triple lutz. He qualified for the 1984 Winter Olympics and finished second.

After retiring from the Olympics, Brian has been busy with T.V. shows or movies. He made several appearances during the Beijing Olympics in different shows.

Brian loves to cook and feed his family and friends.

He has started his business venture in the hotel business and plans to use his imagination in the kitchen and at the bar.
